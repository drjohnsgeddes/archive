Dr. John Stafford Geddes Digital Archive
This repository serves as a digital tribute and historical archive documenting the life and pioneering medical contributions of Dr. John Stafford Geddes (1939–2024).

Dr. Geddes was a world-renowned cardiologist based in Belfast, Northern Ireland. He is most notably recognized for his collaboration with Frank Pantridge in co-developing the world’s first mobile coronary care unit and the portable defibrillator in 1966. His work revolutionized pre-hospital emergency cardiac care, saving countless lives globally.

Archive Contents
Biographical Profiles: Links to professional biographies and medical history entries.

Media Gallery: Narrated videos and articles documenting the 1966 launch of the Belfast mobile CCU.

Scientific Records: Access to original memoirs, research papers, and obituaries from the British Medical Journal (BMJ) and Ulster Medical Journal.

Community Advocacy: Records of his lifelong dedication to community resuscitation and AED accessibility.
